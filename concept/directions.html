Three directions for one calm language

Each is a different answer to the same brief: a calm, judgment-free view of recurring money for someone who avoids finance apps. Same subscription card, three visual languages. Look at all three, then name the one to build into the stand.

The base mechanism is Monzo (flat calm surface, one reserved accent, a quiet status badge, plain-language rows), minus its coral. The clarity row is Apple Wallet. The category chip is Copilot, on a light canvas.

Rejected before shown: red as an accent dark finance canvas mint / sage / beige "calm" reflex rounded-everything + blobs AI-slop (gradient text, glass, eyebrows)

Card radius 14px, 10px buttons. Crisper corners than D1 read as precise, not playful. Form & notes

Card radius 16px, 12px buttons. Plum is a non-red brand hue that reads premium and calm, not alarm. The serif total is the emotional beat; the rest stays quiet sans. This is the direction where the category chip does the most work, so recognition (A4) leads.

All three keep the same structure, copy, and states as the grey Home wireframe. The choice is the language, not the layout. Direction 2, Petrol & Paper, was chosen and built into concept.html, the full stand.
