Customer Journey: To-Be

Emma's ideal path to the same job, inside Tendd. Each step traces to a concrete As-Is barrier and is reconciled with the already-locked structure. Target emotions are projected design goals, not measured.

Emma, 29 · The Anxious Non-Looker
Primary persona × J-MAIN · bank path
When I suspect I am paying for things I forgot about or never use, I want to see all my recurring charges clearly in one calm view, so that I feel in control of my money without needing to become a finance person. personas.md Persona 1; jtbd.md Part 1

The key result of this map: every To-Be step is covered by the already-locked structure (flows.md Flow A, the wireframes, D1 to D5); none contradicts it. The CJM validates that the built structure already covers Emma's As-Is pains, rather than surfacing a gap.

Audit

Risks and reconciliation

Reconciliation (the load-bearing result): every To-Be step is covered by the locked structure (Flow A, the wireframes, D1 to D5), and no step contradicts it. The CJM is the emotional cross-check that confirms the built IA and wireframes already cover Emma's As-Is pains.

Three most dangerous questions

1 H0, not closeable by desk research. The whole MVP core (the B4 + B5 + B6 reveal) delivers a moment whose emotional payoff is unproven with actual avoiders. The clickable-prototype test with 5 to 8 screened avoiders is the gate (strategy.md section 5). The single most consequential open item.
2 Enrichment feasibility (B4). The reveal's quality depends on the enrichment pipeline covering the user's real charges with recognizable names and logos. If coverage is thin, the reveal shows cryptic codes anyway and B5 fails. Partly checkable (Rocket Money uses Plaid enrichment), partly a build-time risk.
3 B7 scoping. Whether the return hook is MVP or LATER for the J-MAIN focus is a scoping call; it is a retention bet beyond H0, and the retention hypotheses it rests on are ? in aarrr.md. Kept MVP for now, flagged for revisit.

Boundary note

The To-Be starts at "Arrive". What brings an avoider to Welcome is the same surprise-charge trigger from As-Is, which the product cannot create; it can only be ready when it happens. How an avoider is acquired is an AARRR Acquisition question outside the in-product journey.
